Michael Jackson

Michael Joseph Jackson (1958 – 2009) was an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and philanthropist. Dubbed the "King of Pop", he is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century. Over a four-decade career, his contributions to music, dance, and fashion, along with his publicized personal life, made him a global figure in popular culture. Jackson influenced artists across many music genres. Through stage and video performances, he popularized street dance moves such as the moonwalk, which he named, and the robot.

Early life and the Jackson 5 (1958–1975)

Michael Jackson, born on August 29, 1958, in Gary, Indiana, was the eighth of ten children in a musical family. He joined the Jackson Brothers, later renamed the Jackson 5, in 1964, where he shared lead vocals and quickly became the main draw. The Jackson 5 signed with Motown in 1969, achieving significant success with hits like "I Want You Back" and "ABC." As a solo artist, Michael released four albums with Motown between 1972 and 1975. Despite their success, the Jackson 5 grew frustrated with Motown's restrictions, leading to creative differences. Michael's early life was marked by strict discipline from his father, contributing to a sense of isolation in his youth.

Artistry

Michael Jackson was influenced by musicians like James Brown, Little Richard, and Diana Ross, with Brown being his greatest inspiration. His vocal technique evolved from boy soprano to lyric tenor, with a range praised for its beauty and emotion. Despite no formal training, Jackson was a proficient composer, using beatboxing and vocal imitations. He was renowned for his dance skills, notably the moonwalk, and incorporated various genres into his music, including pop, soul, funk, and rock. Jackson's music often explored themes of paranoia, social issues, and humanitarianism. His groundbreaking music videos, like "Thriller," redefined the medium and helped break racial barriers on MTV. His work in videos also introduced innovative choreography and special effects, leaving a lasting impact on popular culture.

Honors and awards

Michael Jackson is one of the best-selling music artists in history, with around 500 million records sold worldwide. He had 13 US number-one singles in his solo career and was honored by US presidents three times. Jackson won hundreds of awards, including 13 Grammys, 39 Guinness World Records, and 26 American Music Awards. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice and received numerous other honors, including an honorary doctorate and a posthumous induction into the Rhythm and Blues Music Hall of Fame. Jackson's impact on music and culture is commemorated through various dedications and awards.

Death

On June 25, 2009, Michael Jackson died from cardiac arrest caused by a propofol and benzodiazepine overdose, administered by his personal physician, Conrad Murray. His death, just weeks before his sold-out London concert residency, led to a massive online reaction, causing website crashes and unprecedented web traffic. TV stations worldwide, including MTV and BET, aired marathons of Jackson's music videos and specials in his honor.
